Key Business Findings

1. Promotions are the single biggest lever: Daily promotions drive a 39% sales uplift consistently across all store types. This is the most actionable finding for revenue growth.
2. Promo2 shows diminishing/negative returns: Continuous promotions (Promo2) show a -10.8% effect, suggesting potential promo fatigue. Stores should evaluate whether long-running promotions erode margins without driving incremental sales.
3. Strong December seasonality: December sales spike +24% above average, driven by Christmas shopping. Inventory and staffing plans should peak in Q4.
4. Monday is king: Sun consistently leads weekly sales, possibly due to start-of-week shopping runs. Sat is the weakest day.
5. Store Type b dominates: Type b stores generate the highest average daily sales, likely representing larger-format or premium locations with higher foot traffic and basket sizes.
6. Massive store-to-store variance: Top stores outperform bottom stores by 8x. This variance presents opportunity for operational improvements at underperforming locations.
7. Holiday effects are mixed: Public and Easter holidays show different sales patterns than regular days. Christmas proximity drives the strongest seasonal lift.
8. Competition proximity correlates with higher sales: Stores near competitors tend to be in high-traffic commercial zones, which offsets competitive pressure.
